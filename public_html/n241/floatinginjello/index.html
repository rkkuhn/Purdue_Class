What is it Really Like Living With a Jack Russell?


You have seen TV shows, movies, and commercials with a cute, well-behaved small dog doing everything on cue while being sweet and loving. It is all a lie. They do not tell you that all the hours of training have gone in to make this brilliant, stubborn breed listen. Usually, more than one Jack Russell is doing the same job, and you don't even know the difference. Are they fun-loving? Yes! Are they loyal? Oh yes! Are they stubborn? Oh, big time, like a mule!!!


My seventeen lb Jack Russell is name Palmer. He has been my loyal companion for 11 years now. I adopted him from the Texas Jack Russell Rescue when he was 5 years old. From the time he came into the house, he was a hot little mess. But it hasn't been until recently that he has made it very clear who runs this house. Let me be obvious here, and IT IS NOT ME!


Ever since the pandemic, having me home 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, he figured that would be the new norm. Since going back to school full time, he is a pain in the butt once I get home to study. He will cry, bark, and paw at my leg to get me out of the home office and play with him. I have to stay at school to get my homework and studying completed.


For the most part, Palmer is easy to maintain. Maintenance is a weekly brushing, once a month bath, and lots and lots of play or walks. Palmer is a high-energy dog that does great with kids 7 years and older. Never trust a Jack Russell with children younger than 7. They have eye issues such as cataracts and blindness in the late stages of life. Palmer scratched one eye and almost lost the entire eye. Palmer's eye was saved, but he is 95% blind in that eye, and his other eye has cataracts. Medicated eye drops have to be given to him twice a day. Other than that, his health is excellent. The one thing one will notice with Jack Russell's is they will start to slow down when it starts to get close to the end of their life span. Jack Russell can live 15+ years old, so be prepared for a long-life companion.


The real question is, would I ever get another Jack Russell again. To be honest, yes! So far, I have had three Jack Russells and will get another one once Palmer is no longer playing my little terrorist. With that said, Jack Russell's are not for everyone.
